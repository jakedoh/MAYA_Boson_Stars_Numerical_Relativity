\section{Local Calculation}

We calculate the nonrelativistic and relativistic Bremsstrahlung from Rybicki and Lightman 1983, Eq. 5.15b and 5.25 respectively.  In terms 
of the rest mass density $\rho$ and the internal energy $\epsilon$ in code units, we find the nonrelativistic Bremsstrahlung luminosity to
be 
\begin{equation}
  \varepsilon = 6.86\times10^{34} \rho_c^2 \left((\Gamma-1) f_T \epsilon_c \right)^{1/2}  Z^2 \left( \frac{\bar{g}_B}{1.2} \right) 
	\left( \frac{10^7 M_\odot}{M_c} \right)^4 \left(\mathrm{\frac{ergs}{s\cdot cm^{-3}}} \right)
\end{equation}
where $\Gamma$ is the adiabatic exponent of the equation of state, $f_T = T_e / T_p$ is the ratio of the electron temperature to the proton 
temperature multiplied by the factor $\Gamma-1$ from the ideal gas equation.  $\bar{g}_B$ is the frequency-averaged Gaunt factor, in the 
range of 1.1 - 1.5.  The chosen value of 1.2 introduces a possible 20\% error. $Z$ is the atomic number of the gas atoms (we assume ionized 
hydrogen).

The relativistic version of this Bremsstrahlung luminosity is
\begin{equation}
  \varepsilon = 6.86\times10^{34} \rho_c^2 \left( (\Gamma-1) f_T \epsilon_c \right)^{1/2}  Z^2 \left( \frac{\bar{g}_B}{1.2} \right) 
	\left( \frac{10^7 M_\odot}{M_c} \right)^4 \left( 1 + 4.4\times 10^3 f_T (\Gamma-1) \epsilon_c  \right)
	\left(\mathrm{\frac{ergs}{s\cdot cm^{-3}}} \right)\,.
\end{equation}

\subsection{Integration}

Integrating over a region, we again get factors of the mass.  In $\mathrm{ergs/s}$, the luminosity of an integrated region is:
\begin{eqnarray}
  L_{BS} &=& 2\times10^{71} \left( \frac{\bar{g}_B}{1.2} \right) Z^2 \sqrt{\Gamma-1} f_T^{1/2} \left( \int \rho_c^2 \epsilon_c^{1/2} dV_c \right) \left( \frac{10^7 M_\odot}{M_c}\right ) \\
  L_{RBS} &=& 2\times10^{71} \left( \frac{\bar{g}_B}{1.2} \right) Z^2 \sqrt{\Gamma-1} f_T^{1/2} \left( \int \rho_c^2 \epsilon_c^{1/2} (1+4.4\times10^3 f_T (\Gamma-1) \epsilon_c) dV_c \right) \left( \frac{10^7 M_\odot}{M_c}\right)
\end{eqnarray}
